Tachi

"Nekkyo no shunkan to ora"

Primary-Fire: Art of the Blade. Use your gravity pylons to telekinetically control a floating sword that spins, deflects projectiles, and deals light damage and knockback on contact. Hold Primary-Fire to prime the sword, and release to slingshot it away from the cursor at enemies, dealing moderate damage, knockback, and stun. Impaled enemies are also slowed heavily until the sword is jostled loose by impact force, firing it out as a projectile.

Secondary-Fire: CLIPPED-WING-BUTTERFLY-INSTANT-DEATH-BLUESCREEN CRESENT. Enter KILL MODE, sheathing your sword to dash around at high speeds. Attacking unleashes a C.W.B.I.D.B.S.C., INSTANTLY KILLING anything hit and shattering your sword.

SPECIAL: Blade Master - Killing enemies with swords embedded in them with C.W.B.I.D.B.S.C. re-primes your sheath, allowing you to continue KILL MODE. Duration can be extended as long as there are embedded enemies to kill.

Press this button to THWANG!!!

Overview:

Best Upgrades:

  • Fractured Mind - A heretical technique, once thought lost. Control three blades at the same time, at the cost of your C.W.B.I.D.B.S.C. being weak and innefectual.

  • Harpoon Tip - Impale multiple enemies together with one stab, locking them in place and allowing you to kill both at once with one C.W.B.I.D.B.S.C.

  • Ricochet Simulation - Channel your inner Jedi, and reflect bullets directly back at their sources rather than randomly.

Trivia:

  • According to Word of God from the RAM 1.0 devlog, the gimmick of embedding swords in enemies was inspired by a scene from Chainsaw Man.
  • The Forbidden Oathblade weapon line from the Terraria Calamity Mod was based off of Tachi. These reworks were added in the Brainstorm Update, which was actually delayed in part because of how many devs were too busy playing RAM to work on the mod.
  • Never bring a plasma gun to a plasma sword fight.

Collider

A wise football coach once said:

The best offense is a good defense... that also coincidentally acts as an offense (by virtue of absorbing bullets and firing them.)

Primary-Fire: Shotgun. Fires a spread of 6 pellets, each dealing 9 damage.

Secondary-Fire: Resonance Hammer. Charge up a short-range melee attack that can deflect projectiles and bullets. Deals 30 damage.

SPECIAL: Firing your shotgun right as Resonance Hammer is unleashed activates a Naildriver, massively reducing spread. Missing the window instead results in a Scattershot, increasing spread instead. All boosted pellets deal 1.7X damage, and move faster.

Overview:

Steeltoes are simple to use hosts that can deal consistent damage at range. Naildrivers and Scattershots offset the otherwise low damage of the shotgun, and can be used to set up powerful Trickshots. Aditionally, out of all of the bots that can reflect projectiles, Steeltoes have some of the best reflecting capability. Resonance-Hammered Router grenades deal great impact damage, as do enemy Steeltoe bullets. Their main weaknesses are their severe lack of splash damage, and their relative frailty, not having the physical bulk of Deadlifts or the movement speed and I-Frames of Routers.

Best Upgrades:

  • Induction Barrel - Sets your pellets on fire, converting them into piercing flames!

  • Stacked Shells - Surely more shells can fit in the gun, right? Gain an extra barrel (or three), and unload all of them at once!

  • Ricochet Shells - Bullets bounce off walls. What kind of idiot thinks bullets don't bounce off walls?

Trivia:

  • Steeltoes clearly draw design inspiration from V1 from Ultrakill, especially in regards to their projectile boosting deflect, and their triangular heads.
  • The design for Steeltoes in pre-release RAM was different, being bulkier and having a badass cloak. I've personally christened these early Steeltoes "Irongreaves", but that is just my silly fanon name.
  • They're arguably the most prominent RAM bot, being featured on the game's box art as well as having one of the more in-depth and complicated questlines. So much for "RAM is the roguelike with no main character", huh?
  • That being said, out of every RAM bot, they've gotten the least attention from Xylem Studios during their devlogs. So maybe they aren't the favorite of Toby and Andrew after all...
